# Sources

Every substantive claim traces to a primary or clearly-tiered source. Cite the tier in answers — that
transparency is the skill's credibility. Verified July 2026; this area changes fast (especially tracking
and state laws), so date-check before relying on any time-sensitive item.

## Tier 1 — Statute, regulation & court record (strongest)
- HIPAA Privacy Rule, 45 CFR Part 164 — marketing definition (164.501), marketing authorization
  (164.508(a)(3)), psychotherapy notes (164.501, 164.508(a)(2)), minimum necessary (164.502(b), 164.514(d)),
  de-identification (164.514(b)) — via eCFR (ecfr.gov) and HHS.
- 45 CFR 160.103 (business associate definition); 164.502(e)/164.504(e) (BAA requirements) — eCFR.
- 42 CFR Part 2 — substance-use-disorder records (2024 final rule aligning with HIPAA).
- *American Hospital Association v. Becerra*, N.D. Tex., June 20, 2024 — vacated part of the OCR tracking
  bulletin (referenced in HHS's own updated bulletin).

## Tier 2 — HHS OCR & FTC primary guidance/enforcement (authoritative)
- HHS OCR "Use of Online Tracking Technologies" bulletin — hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/privacy/guidance/hipaa-online-tracking (orig. Dec 2022; updated Mar 18, 2024; reviewed June 26, 2024).
- HHS OCR: business associates guidance; de-identification guidance; cloud computing guidance (BAA required for CSPs; de-identified data exempt).
- FTC enforcement (press releases + orders on ftc.gov): GoodRx (Feb 1, 2023, $1.5M), Premom (May 17, 2023, $100K), BetterHelp (order July 14, 2023, $7.8M), Cerebral (Apr 15, 2024, $7M+), Monument (Apr 2024).
- FTC Health Breach Notification Rule + 2024 amendments (finalized Apr 26, 2024; Federal Register 2024-10855, May 30, 2024; effective July 29, 2024).
- FTC Office of Technology, "Lurking Beneath the Surface" pixel analysis (Mar 16, 2023).

## Tier 4 — Secondary legal analysis (interpretation only; not primary)
Law-firm and compliance-vendor analysis (WilmerHale, Ropes & Gray, Holland & Knight, McDermott, HIPAA
Journal, etc.) used only to interpret the primary sources above. Never cite these as the rule itself.
